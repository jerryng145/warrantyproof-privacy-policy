Why electronics and appliances are easy to lose warranty proof for

Electronics and appliances often come with receipts, order confirmations, serial numbers, manuals, warranty cards, and support messages in different places. When a device fails months later, the proof needed for a repair or replacement can be hard to reconstruct quickly.

What to save for a future warranty claim

  • Receipt or order confirmation
  • Purchase date and retailer name
  • Warranty length and expiry date
  • Serial number and model number
  • Product photos, manuals, and support notes

Warranty tracker vs photo album

A photo album can hold receipt photos, but it usually does not connect those photos to warranty dates, serial numbers, retailer notes, manuals, or reminder-oriented product records. WarrantyProof is designed around the full proof record, not only the image.

FAQ

What electronics and appliances should I track?

Common examples include phones, laptops, tablets, cameras, televisions, coffee machines, refrigerators, washers, dryers, vacuum cleaners, tools, and other products where a receipt, serial number, or warranty date may matter later.

Can WarrantyProof store proof of purchase?

WarrantyProof is designed to help organize proof of purchase and claim-ready product records. Users should still review saved details before relying on them for support, refund, warranty, or insurance requests.

Does WarrantyProof upload receipts to a server?

The public privacy positioning is local-first: no account, no ads, no tracking, and no app-owned server upload. Records stay on device unless the user exports or shares them.

Does WarrantyProof guarantee a warranty claim?

No. WarrantyProof helps organize proof and reminders, but claim decisions are made by the retailer, manufacturer, service provider, or insurer.
