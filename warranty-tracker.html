What is a warranty tracker?

A warranty tracker is a tool for keeping receipts, purchase dates, retailer details, warranty expiry dates, serial numbers, product photos, manuals, and claim notes together.

Who needs a warranty tracker?

A warranty tracker is useful for people who want one place to organize important purchase records for electronics, appliances, tools, devices, furniture, and other items where proof may matter later.

Warranty tracker vs spreadsheet

A spreadsheet can list warranty dates, but it often separates the receipt image, serial number photo, manual, and claim notes into other folders. WarrantyProof is designed around the full proof record.

Warranty tracker vs photo album

A photo album can store receipt photos, but it usually does not connect those photos to warranty expiry dates, model numbers, retailer notes, documents, and reminders.

What to save for a warranty claim

  • Receipt or order confirmation
  • Purchase date and retailer name
  • Warranty length and expiry date
  • Serial number and model number
  • Product photos, manuals, support notes, and claim notes

Electronics and appliance warranty tracking

Electronics and appliances are common warranty-tracking use cases because receipts, serial numbers, manuals, and support messages are often scattered across different places.

FAQ

Can WarrantyProof organize proof of purchase?

WarrantyProof is designed to help organize proof of purchase and claim-ready product records. Users should still review saved details before relying on them for support, refund, warranty, or insurance requests.

Does WarrantyProof upload receipts to a server?

The public privacy positioning is local-first: no account, no ads, no tracking, and no app-owned server upload. Records stay on device unless the user exports or shares them.

Is WarrantyProof an accounting app?

No. WarrantyProof is a receipt and warranty organizer. It should not be described as an accounting expense platform, tax tool, insurance product, or legal adviser.

Does WarrantyProof guarantee a warranty claim?

No. WarrantyProof helps organize proof and reminders, but claim decisions are made by the retailer, manufacturer, service provider, or insurer.
